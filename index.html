Origin Of MUJI

MUJI in Japanese is Mujirushi Ryohin which translates to "no-brand quality goods" . MUJI, originally founded in 1980 in Japan, offers consumers a wide variety of products including Stationary, Household Goods, Apparel, and Food. Muji is based on its 3 core principles:

  • 1. Selection of materials
  • 2. Streamlining of processes
  • 3. Simplification of packages

    • MUJI isn't in the range of minimalist style rather it is more of a concise style. MUJIs origin comes from a process of trying to create simple, low-cost, good quality products. MUJI's goal is to be able to provide consumers with a rational satisfaction, expressed with a "this will do" rather than a "this is what I really want". By doing so, MUJI aims to be something that is enough and not something that you really want. MUJI's ideas for their products is not for their products to scream MUJI. The idea itself is the product and MUJI delivers it to their coonsumers. MUJI aims to live up to everyone's expectations.

    Reason Why People Should Shop At MUJI


    There are many reason why you should shop at MUJI
    MUJI is a brand that offers products with simplicity and sustainability. Their products leave less waste than other brands do and their products tend to have a natural appeal.
    MUJI's products can be used in different and useful ways. For example, their pens are refillable, or like their 90-degree socks that don't slip and so on. MUJI lets customers to tailor their products towards their own liking. For example, when you buy their notebooks in-store, you get to design the notebook with stamps at their stamp station.
    MUJI offers a wide variety of products at a low-cost with good quality. This is the perfect option for a lot of people including people who may live alone or with others.
