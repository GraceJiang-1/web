Origin Of MUJI

MUJI in Japanese is Mujirushi Ryohin which translates to "no-brand quality goods" . MUJI, originally founded in 1980 in Japan, offers consumers a wide variety of products including stationary, household goods, apparel, and food. Muji is based on its 3 core principles:

  • 1. Selection of materials
  • 2. Streamlining of processes
  • 3. Simplification of packages

    • MUJI isn't in the range of minimalist style rather it is more of a concise style. MUJIs origin comes from a process of trying to create simple, low-cost, good quality products. MUJI's goal is to be able to provide consumers with a rational satisfaction, expressed with a "this will do" rather than a "this is what I really want". By doing so, MUJI aims for consumers to be able to trust their products rather than consumers expressing distrust or self-interest. MUJI aims to live up to everyone's expectations.
